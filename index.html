Harry Potter

The Magical Journey of The Boy Who Lived — 2001–2011

A cinematic saga of friendship, courage, and sacrifice

Series Synopsis (Scroll)

The Harry Potter saga follows an orphaned boy who discovers he is a wizard and is introduced to a hidden magical world. Over eight films Harry matures from curious child to leader of a resistance against Lord Voldemort. Friendship, sacrifice, and the cost of love drive the story forward as Hogwarts itself becomes a living, breathing character. The saga weaves wonder, danger, and heartbreak across dozens of unforgettable set pieces. It culminates in a final struggle where ordinary courage meets extraordinary destiny.

Characters

Harry Potter Trio

Harry Potter

The Boy Who Lived — brave, compassionate, and destined to face Voldemort. He learns leadership, loss and the value of choices.

Hermione Granger

Brilliant witch and the trio's strategist — fiercely moral, resourceful, and devoted to equality for all magical folk.

Ron Weasley

Loyal best friend — warm, humorous and courageous in his own, grounded way. Family and friendship define him.

Hogwarts Staff & Allies

Albus Dumbledore

Headmaster of Hogwarts — wise, complex, and willing to shoulder hard decisions for the greater good.

Minerva McGonagall

Deputy Headmistress — stern, principled and fiercely protective of her students and Hogwarts.

Severus Snape

Potions Master with complex loyalties — his tragic past and secret devotion reshape the narrative.

Rubeus Hagrid

Keeper of Keys & Grounds — giant-hearted, devoted to magical creatures and to Harry like family.

Remus Lupin

Defence Against the Dark Arts professor — compassionate, wise and burdened by lycanthropy.

Sybill Trelawney

Divination professor — eccentric yet linked to key prophecies in the saga.

Weasley Family

Ginny Weasley

Skilled witch and Harry’s love interest — brave, independent and a strong fighter.

Fred Weasley

Inventive twin, prankster and co-founder of Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes.

George Weasley

Fred’s twin, business partner, and comic courage in dark times.

Arthur Weasley

Curious Ministry worker fascinated by Muggles, devoted to family and decency.

Molly Weasley

Maternal and fierce — a loving hearth and a fearsome defender of family.

Percy Weasley

Ambitious Ministry official who learns the price of pride and the value of forgiveness.

Bill Weasley

Gringotts curse-breaker — pragmatic, brave and world-aware.

Charlie Weasley

Dragon expert — adventurous and devoted to magical creatures abroad.

Order of the Phoenix

Sirius Black

Harry’s godfather — reckless, beloved and tragically lost to war.

Alastor “Mad-Eye” Moody

Legendary Auror — battle-hardened and tireless in defense of others.

Nymphadora Tonks

Metamorphmagus Auror — playful, loyal and tragically devoted in love.

Kingsley Shacklebolt

Calm Auror who later helps restore justice at the Ministry.

Students (Other Houses)

Neville Longbottom

Grows from timid pupil into courageous leader — a late-blooming hero.

Luna Lovegood

Quirky Ravenclaw — kind, perceptive and quietly brave.

Cho Chang

Ravenclaw Seeker, Harry’s early love interest — complicated by grief.

Cedric Diggory

Noble Hufflepuff — fair, brave and tragically young when lost.

Draco Malfoy

Privileged Slytherin — rival to Harry, shaped by family and fear.

Pansy Parkinson

Slytherin student — loyal within her social circle and often harsh.

Villains & Other Key Figures

Lord Voldemort

Dark Lord — obsessed with immortality and domination; the central antagonist.

Bellatrix Lestrange

Fanatic Death Eater — cruel, volatile and frighteningly devoted.

Lucius Malfoy

Wealthy Death Eater — manipulative and politically ambitious.

Peter Pettigrew

Betrayer of Harry’s parents — a coward plastered in false loyalty.

Barty Crouch Jr.

Deceptive Death Eater — one of Voldemort’s cruelest conspirators.

Dobby

Free house-elf — small, brave and selfless, fiercely devoted to Harry.

Kreacher

House-elf of the Black family — surly but ultimately helpful in key moments.

Films — Summaries, Runtime & Ratings

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone (2001)

Harry discovers he is a wizard and arrives at Hogwarts. He befriends Ron and Hermione and learns about his past and fame. Strange events swirl around a mystical stone. The trio navigates traps and tests to protect it from dark forces. A first confrontation with Voldemort sets Harry’s destiny. Wonder and danger open the door to a larger war ahead.

Runtime: 2h 32m

Release Date: November 16, 2001

Customer Rating: 8.1 / 10 — ★★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002)

Second year at Hogwarts brings mysterious attacks and a legend of a hidden chamber. Harry finds a diary that reveals a darker youth of Tom Riddle. The trio discovers secret passages and faces a basilisk in deadly subterranean halls. The story reveals early ties to Voldemort’s past and the danger of unchecked prejudice. Courage and cleverness save Ginny and the school’s future.

Runtime: 2h 41m

Release Date: November 15, 2002

Customer Rating: 7.4 / 10 — ★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (2004)

Sirius Black escapes Azkaban and is rumored to be hunting Harry. Dementors darken the halls of Hogwarts, adding terror and dread. Time-travel and revelations reveal betrayal and sacrifice in Harry’s past circle. Lupin’s mentorship deepens the emotional core, and the truth reshapes loyalties. The film blends gothic atmosphere with heartfelt character work.

Runtime: 2h 22m

Release Date: June 4, 2004

Customer Rating: 8.0 / 10 — ★★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (2005)

Harry is unwillingly entered into the perilous Triwizard Tournament. Tasks test his courage, skill and morality across dragons, merfolk and mazes. International wizarding communities and duels add political size to the story. The tournament’s finale leads to Voldemort’s terrifying rebirth. The tone shifts as adolescence collides with real, dark threats.

Runtime: 2h 37m

Release Date: November 18, 2005

Customer Rating: 7.7 / 10 — ★★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (2007)

The Ministry denies Voldemort’s return, sowing confusion and censorship. Harry forms Dumbledore’s Army to prepare students for resistance. Dolores Umbridge’s authoritarian rule at Hogwarts spurs rebellion and danger. A vision-driven plot leads to a devastating clash at the Ministry of Magic. The film deepens loss as personal stakes become political, and Harry learns leadership’s loneliness.

Runtime: 2h 18m

Release Date: July 11, 2007

Customer Rating: 7.5 / 10 — ★★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2009)

Dumbledore and Harry delve into Voldemort’s past to find Horcrux clues. Teenage romance and jealousy thread through quiet dread and brewing war. A found textbook and secret identity change how the trio navigates school. Trust fractures as danger closes in on Hogwarts. A shocking loss shakes the wizarding world, accelerating the final confrontation.

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1 (2010)

The trio leaves Hogwarts and hunts Horcruxes in hiding — on the run and under constant threat. Deprivation, betrayal, and secrets strain the bond between friends. The legend of the Deathly Hallows tempts with perilous promises. Losses mount as Voldemort consolidates power over the world. Quiet heroism and small resistances shape the film’s weary, powerful tone.

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2 (2011)

The final battle for Hogwarts erupts, uniting allies against Voldemort’s forces. Secrets — especially Snape’s — are finally revealed, reframing many lives. Horcruxes are destroyed at great personal cost, and heroes fall. Harry faces Voldemort in an ultimate duel that resolves prophecy and sacrifice. The saga closes with loss, healing and a hopeful epilogue.

Behind the Magic

Chris Columbus

Director (Sorcerer's Stone & Chamber of Secrets). Brought the tone of wonder and warmth to the first films, grounding the world with heart.

Focused on faithful visualisation of Rowling’s world and guiding the young cast in their breakout roles.

J.K. Rowling

Author of the Harry Potter books — created the layered world of Hogwarts, magical rules, and deep character arcs.

Her storytelling balances whimsy with weighty themes — love, loss, identity, and moral choice.

The books inspired the films and continue to influence global popular culture and new generations of readers.
